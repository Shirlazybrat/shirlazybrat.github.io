What events led you to decide that you want to be a developer?

After completing my master's degree in math education, a series of events caused me to rethink the future of my current career path so I began to analyze the circumstances that I would possibly face. Not being content with the possible outcomes, I began to look for careers that would allow me to learn and grow in an industry that will encourage certain freedoms and experiences that don't readily exist in other careers. I learned about coding from a friend's post on Facebook and completed some research more into what it meant to be a coder and how one could make a career of it. I was particularly drawn to the statistics around the lack of diversity in the tech industry. Approximately only 1% of web developers are black women. That knowledge alone made me seriously consider how I could make a difference in the tech industry while doing something that I can grow to love. Following my in-depth research, I made the decision to pursue a career as a web developer. I want to use technology to create resources that will better the lives of others.

Tell me about a time you had to solve a complex problem. How did you break down the problem into smaller units to create a solution?

My most complex problem came as a result of my dilemma after officially completing my master's degree program. Once I realized that there was a life-altering, and expensive, issue to resolve, I immediately began to collect my thoughts by applying logic and reasoning to how I could fix this issue. Using my immediate thoughts, I went to the internet to research possible avenues that I could take. These possibilities were utilized during the creation of my mind map, where I wrote out possible options for what I would decide to do. Mind mapping led me to reach out to a few experts with specific inquiries. Their responses would be significant in my decision making process, providing me with facts and details that would play in role in my decision making process. In order to stick to the timeline established for reaching a solution, I combined all of my research and options to a set of approximately five plans labeled A through E, E being worst case scenario. Each plan was ordered sequentially with considerations 4 time, finance, and resources needed, which were also implemented into each plan. By completing this systematic approach to my problem, I was able to make the best decision, weighing out every possible alternative, while also creating realistic alternatives.

Why are you interested in learning web development? Where does your motivation come from?

Dedication, focus, determination, desire, resilience, and passion. These are the words that come to mind when I think of a bootcamp/coding school student. These are the qualities that I possess as I work towards pursuing a career as a web developer. After making the decision to change careers, I have dedicated my time completely to my learning, putting my social life on hold, as I worked consistently to gain, and retain, knowledge that will help me to move forward as a future web developer. I have, and will continue to work tirelessly to gain skills and understanding of syntax, frameworks, and methods needed for programming. My desire is to create and build applications that can be used by other people to make life easier, to solve problems using technology. This desire is what encourages me to keep growing, to work through my frustrations during my learning, never giving up. This same desire fuels my determination to pursue and achieve success. I can, and I will, be a successful web developer. I am focused on my future and how my creations will impact someone in a way that will improve their life. That's a big deal. My passion to become a “builder of things” is the driving force behind my work ethic, encouraging my resilience in the tech industry. As a person with little to no technological background, I realize that I have much to learn and am willing to go above and beyond expectations to achieve my goal. For these reasons, I believe that I am a strong candidate for any potential coding bootcamp.
